# Responsive Layout Constitution

Updated: 2026-04-23
Status: Mandatory layout law for browser-adaptive workspace surfaces.

## 1. Layout Authority

### 1.1 Clause IDs

- `UX-RSP-001`: Gulf Hisab shall adapt intelligently to browser width and height.
- `UX-RSP-002`: Layout shall reflow without destroying task continuity.
- `UX-RSP-003`: Responsive behavior shall be rule-based rather than left to CSS drift.

### 1.2 Responsive behavior

- `UX-RSP-010`: Tables, cards, panels, and preview windows shall behave predictably across viewport sizes.
- `UX-RSP-011`: Multi-panel work areas shall collapse or reflow according to declared behavior.
- `UX-RSP-012`: Browser resize shall not break hierarchy, overflow logic, or preview usability.

### 1.3 Density discipline

- `UX-RSP-020`: No oversized spacing, dead empty areas, or mega-card waste shall be allowed in operational work areas.
- `UX-RSP-021`: Dense financial and productivity SaaS behavior shall be the default.
- `UX-RSP-022`: Responsive reduction shall preserve utility, not merely hide content.

## 2. Viewport Rules

- `UX-RSP-030`: Large screens shall support multi-panel productivity where declared.
- `UX-RSP-031`: Medium screens shall preserve task sequencing by collapsing secondary regions first.
- `UX-RSP-032`: Small screens shall maintain record access, edit access, and back-navigation clarity.
