# Reconciliation Constitution

## 1. Purpose
1.1 Reconciliation shall continuously verify consistency across modules.
1.2 Reconciliation shall operate as a governed verification layer, not an optional review.

## 2. Verification Targets
2.1 Journal versus report.
2.2 Stock versus accounting.
2.3 VAT versus invoices.
2.4 Balances versus payment and allocation truth.

## 3. Cycle Law
3.1 Reconciliation shall run in defined cycles.
3.2 Each cycle shall classify outcomes by severity.
3.3 Mismatch shall be captured with evidence and root-cause zones.

## 4. Failure Law
4.1 Reconciliation shall fail explicitly on unreconciled mismatch.
4.2 No hidden failure shall be allowed.

## 5. Clause IDs
5.1 Stable clauses in this document use the prefix `RECON-CON`.
5.2 Future control points and audits shall reference the exact clause IDs defined here.

## 6. Enforceable Clauses
- `RECON-CON-001`: Reconciliation shall continuously verify defined targets.
- `RECON-CON-002`: Reconciliation shall classify mismatch by severity.
- `RECON-CON-003`: Reconciliation shall retain evidence.
- `RECON-CON-004`: Reconciliation shall fail explicitly on mismatch.
- `RECON-CON-005`: Hidden failure shall be forbidden.
