# UX Interaction Constitution

Updated: 2026-04-23
Status: Mandatory interaction law for workspace surfaces.

## 1. Interaction Authority

### 1.1 Clause IDs

- `UX-INT-001`: Interaction behavior shall be explicit, intentional, and auditable.
- `UX-INT-002`: Hover, focus, active, loading, empty, error, success, and disabled states shall be governed.
- `UX-INT-003`: Interaction state shall not drift between screens, components, or responsive breakpoints.

### 1.2 Pointer and control law

- `UX-INT-010`: Pointer and cursor behavior shall be explicit for interactive elements.
- `UX-INT-011`: Buttons, tabs, segmented controls, chips, filters, and floating actions shall follow one interaction system.
- `UX-INT-012`: Interaction transitions shall be purposeful, minimal, premium, and fast.

### 1.3 Workflow clarity

- `UX-INT-020`: The user shall always know where they are, what record they are viewing, what can be edited, how to go back, and whether changes are saved.
- `UX-INT-021`: Blocking overlays and floating helpers shall not interfere with business workflows.
- `UX-INT-022`: A helper widget shall not trap the user or hide the primary task path.

## 2. State Discipline

- `UX-INT-030`: Loading states shall be visible and deterministic.
- `UX-INT-031`: Error states shall identify the failed interaction and not merely show a generic message.
- `UX-INT-032`: Success states shall confirm what changed and whether the change is persisted.
- `UX-INT-033`: Disabled states shall explain why the action is unavailable when that explanation is required for safe execution.
